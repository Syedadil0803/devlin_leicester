🛠️ Get It Right from the Ground Up: Your Essential Guide to Floor Preparation

May 08, 2025
  • by Michael Devlin
  • 5-7 minutes read

📝 Summary

Planning new flooring? Discover how to prepare your subfloor like a pro with this UK-based guide. Avoid costly mistakes and ensure a smooth, long-lasting finish.

Skipping floor prep is like skipping sleep — it catches up with you. Taking time to prep well means your floor looks better, feels better, and lasts longer.

💡 Why Floor Preparation Is Non-Negotiable

Flooring success starts before the first plank or roll is even laid. Without proper preparation, your new floor could wear unevenly, squeak, or fail prematurely.

Think of it as building a house — you wouldn't skip the foundation

This blog gives you the step-by-step lowdown on floor preparation—what's involved, why it matters, and how to get it right.

Step 1: Clear and Clean the Space

• Move furniture and remove old flooring materials
• Clean thoroughly — dust and debris can interfere with adhesives..
• Address staples, nails, and leftover glue.

💡 Tip: Leave samples overnight and check them under natural and artificial light.

Step 2: Check for Moisture

Damp subfloors are one of the biggest reasons floors fail.

• Use a moisture meter or consult a flooring professional.
• Ensure concrete is fully cured (usually 60+ days).
• Consider a DPM (damp proof membrane) for peace of mind.

Especially important for basements or ground-level rooms in the UK.

Step 3: Level the Subfloor

Uneven floors can lead to lifting, bouncing, or visible ridges.

• Concrete: May need self-levelling compound or screed.
• Timber: Fix loose boards, replace damaged sections, or add plywood overlay.
• Ensure flatness within ±3mm over 2m (British Standard BS8203).

Step 4: Choose the Right Underlay

Different flooring needs different underlays:

• Carpet: PU foam or felt underlay for softness and insulation.
• Laminate/LVT: : Thin foam underlay with vapour barrier.
• Vinyl sheet: May need hardboard or smoothing compound.
• Underfloor heating? Use low TOG-rated underlay.

Step 5: Let the Flooring Acclimatise

Most floors need time to adjust to your room:

• Leave in the room 48 hours before fitting.
• Keep heating and ventilation at normal levels.
• This reduces risks of expansion or shrinkage after fitting.

Step 6: Plan for Height Differences

New flooring may sit higher or lower than previous materials:

• Check door clearance — doors may need trimming.
• Plan for threshold strips or ramps at room transitions.
• Consider impact on skirting boards and kitchen plinths.

Step 7: Talk to Your Fitter (or Us!)

A smooth floor starts with good communication. Ask:

• Will they prep the floor or do you need to?
• Are they checking levels and moisture before laying?
• Do they provide guidance on aftercare?

🛠️ Floor Type–Specific Prep Advice (Step-by-Step)

Choosing the right flooring is only part of the story. The way you prepare your subfloor can make or break the final result. Here's how to get it right—for every major flooring type we offer.

🧸 Carpet

Best For: Bedrooms, Living Rooms, Stairs

Step-by-Step Prep:

• 🧹 Remove any existing carpet and underlay
• 🧼 Clean the subfloor thoroughly
• 💧 Check for moisture, especially on concrete floors
• 🔧 Repair creaky boards or cracks
• 🔨 Install gripper rods around the edges
• 🧽 Lay underlay (8–11mm PU foam recommended.
• 🧷 Fit the carpet using stretch-fit technique

👉 Pro Tip: Hire a professional for a seamless finish and proper gripper installation

🪄 Luxury Vinyl Tiles (LVT)

Best For: Kitchens, Bathrooms, Hallways

Step-by-Step Prep:

• 🧽 Remove existing floor coverings
• 🧹 Clean thoroughly—LVT needs a spotless base
• 🔁 Level the subfloor with screed (2–4mm usually)
• 💧 Test for moisture (75% RH on concrete)
• 🧪 Prime before installation
• 🧩 Lay tiles once cured and dry

👉 Pro Tip: Levelling is technical—this one's best left to the pros.

🧰 Laminate

Best For: Living Rooms, Dining Areas, Offices

Step-by-Step Prep:

• 🔨 Remove any previous flooring
• 🧼 Vacuum and clean
• 📏 Check level (gaps >3mm should be corrected)
• 🧽 Install underlay (2–5mm foam or fibreboard)
• DPM needed for concrete
• 📐 Add spacers for 10mm expansion
• 🧩 Click-lay boards after 48 hours acclimation

👉 DIY Friendly: Many homeowners enjoy laying laminate themselves.

🌳 Engineered Wood

Best For: Hallways, Lounges, Open-plan Living

Step-by-Step Prep:

• 🧱 Identify subfloor (plywood, chipboard, concrete)
• 🧼 Clean and test moisture levels
• 🧰 Level if needed
• • 🧽 Choose underlay (if floating) – 3–5mm acoustic or fibreboard
• 🧴 Apply adhesive if gluing
• 🧩 Lay boards with 10mm expansion gaps

👉 Professional Recommended: This is a premium product—expert fit ensures longevity.

🌿 Artificial Grass (Indoor & Outdoor)

Outdoor Prep:

• 🌱 Remove turf and dig 50–100mm
• 🧱 Lay MOT Type 1 + sand, compact both
• 🌾 Add weed membrane
• 🧷 Lay & secure grass with adhesive or pins

Indoor Prep:

• 🧼 Clean & level subfloor
• 🧽 Lay shockpad underlay (10–20mm)
• 🧴 Fix grass with tackifier or double-sided tape

👉 DIY Friendly: Especially for small indoor spaces. Outdoors? Best left to landscapers.

🪵 Natural Flooring (Sisal, Seagrass, Coir)

Best For: Bedrooms, Hallways (Dry Areas Only)

Step-by-Step Prep:

• 🔨 Remove old coverings
• 🧼 Clean and dry the subfloor
• 📏 Install plywood or hardboard overlay
• 🧽 Lay breathable underlay
• Thickness: 6–9mm
• 🧴 Apply tackifier adhesive
• 🧩 Lay natural flooring carefully

👉 Professional Required: These natural fibres can fray or warp if mishandled.

✨ The perfect floor starts with what you can't see—good preparation means beautiful, long-lasting results you'll love every day.
